Spectral radius and the norm of self-adjoint operator

Let $X, Y$ be Hilbert spaces and let $T \in \mathcal{B}(X, Y)$ be a self-adjoint operator. I would like to prove that $||T|| = r(T)$

First, applying the C*-identity, we claim that $||T^{*}T||=||T||$, then by induction $||T^{2^{n}}||=||T||^{2^{n}}$. The next step is to apply the Berling formula: indeed $$r(T) = \lim_{n \to \infty}{||T^{n}||^{\frac{1}{n}}}=\lim_{n \to \infty}{||T^{2n}||^{\frac{1}{2n}}}=||T||$$

Spectrum of an idempotent element

Lemma: Let $A$ be a unital algebra and $a \in A$ be an idempotenent element, i.e. satisfiying the $a^{2}=a$. Then the $\sigma(a) = \{0, 1 \}$. Proof: Let's consider the $f(x) = x-x^{2}$ Since the polynomial calculus preserves the spectrum, i.e. $\sigma(f(a))=f(\sigma(a))$, $\sigma(f(a))=\sigma(a-a^{2})=\sigma(0)=0$, on the other hand, $f(\sigma(a))=\sigma(a)-\sigma(a)^{2}=0$, then $\sigma(a) = \{0, 1 \}$.

Schauder compactness theorem

Theorem: (Schauder compactness theorem) Let $T \in \mathcal{B}(X, Y)$, then $T$ is a compact operator iff $T^{*}$ (Banach adjoint) is a compact operator.

Dini theorem

This is a small note regarding the classical Dini theorem, which establishes the condition in which pointwise convergence impies unifrom.

Dini theorem Let $X$ be a compact metric space and let $\{ f_{n} \}$ be a monotonically increasing sequence ($f_{n}(x) \leq f_{n+1}(x))$ for each $x \in X$). Then if the pointwise limit is continuous, then the sequence converges uniformly.

Proof Let $\epsilon > 0$ given. Let $g_{n} = f -f_{n}$ for each $n$ and let $E_{n}$ be a set of all $x$ such that $|g_{n}(x)| < \epsilon$. Since $g$ is continuous, then each $E_{n}$ is open. Since $f_{n}$ is monotonically increasing, then $g_{n}$ is decresing, then $\{ E_{n} \}$ is ascending. $\{E_{n} \}$ is an open cover of X, since $f_{n} \rightarrow f$ pointwise. Then, by compactness of $X$, there exists an finite subcover. Since $\{ E_{n} \}$ is ascending, then the biggest of all the elements of the cover is a cover of $X$, in other words, for some $N$ $\{E_{N}\}$ is a cover of $X$. Then, if $n > N, x \in X$, then $|f_{n}(x)-f(x)| < \epsilon$, as desired.
